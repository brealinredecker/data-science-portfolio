<h1> Project Description </h1> <br>

This project explores the question: which fast food restaurant is actually the healthiest? I gathered nutritional data from four major chains—McDonald’s, Burger King, Chick-fil-A, and Wendy’s—and cleaned, standardized, and merged the datasets into a single structured dataset for analysis. <br>

To make meaningful comparisons, I created a custom “health score” that weighted calories, fat, and sodium to quantify how healthy or unhealthy each menu item is. This allowed me to rank items within each restaurant and directly compare menus across brands in a consistent way. <br>

Using data visualization, I analyzed patterns such as the distribution of health scores, relationships between calories and fat, and differences across food categories like burgers and chicken. The results challenged common assumptions—for example, restaurants typically seen as “healthier” did not always perform better when measured quantitatively, and portion sizes and protein levels added important context to the findings. <br>

Overall, this project highlights my ability to take a real-world question, build a clean dataset, engineer meaningful metrics, and communicate insights through clear and engaging visual storytelling. <br>
